# 2026 Market Context

What's true RIGHT NOW that changes what to build. Strategic lens, not just data.

## The landscape (verified signals — updated March 2026)

### AI agents are the platform
- Salesforce Agentforce: $540M ARR, 18,500 customers (fastest-growing product)
- Every SaaS company shipping "AI agents" — most are wrappers, few deliver real value
- MCP (Model Context Protocol): 1,000+ community servers, OpenAI adopted 2025, de facto standard
- Claude Code marketplace: 340+ plugins, 89K installs on top skill
- Multi-agent system inquiries surged 1,445% from Q1 2024 to Q2 2025
- 144 non-human identities per human employee in average enterprise
- The gap: everyone builds agents that WRITE code. Nobody builds agents that THINK about products.

### The frame shift: sell the work, not the tool
- Sequoia: "If you sell the tool, you're in a race against the model. If you sell the work, every improvement in the model makes your service faster, cheaper, and harder to compete with."
- "The next trillion-dollar company will be a software company masquerading as a services firm."
- Copilots → Autopilots. Assistants → Colleagues. Tools → Finished work.
- YC W26 examples: Corvera ($0→$33K MRR in 4 weeks — AI agent workforce), Foaster (AI-native consulting — agents do analytical work)

### YC W26 batch signals
- 196 companies. ~60% AI. 14% physical products (deliberate counterweight to software-only).
- Demo Day: March 24, 2026. Deal: $500K for 7%.
- RFS priorities: Cursor for PMs, AI-Native Hedge Funds, AI-Native Agencies, Stablecoin Finance, AI for Government, Modern Metal Mills, AI for Physical Work, Large Spatial Models, Government Fraud Infra, Make LLMs Easy to Train
- Notable: VOYAGR (mapping for agents, not humans), TesterArmy (AI on every PR), Cardinal (40+ YC companies paying before batch started)
- Paul Graham (March 2026): "You've got to find people who want what you're making A LOT. That's necessarily going to be a small number at first."

### Verification > Generation
- Anthropic's own guide: verification skills are "#1 worth spending a week on"
- Agentic coding trends: "verification lattice" (gate-based state machines) is top pattern
- Amplitude shipped 5 monitoring agents (Feb 2026) — continuous, not point-in-time
- The shift: 2024 was "AI generates code." 2026 is "AI proves the code works."
- Rescue engineering emerging: vibe-coded products can't support real usage. CodeScene: AI coding assistants increase defect risk by 30%+ in poor code health projects.

### Solo founders are the new normal
- AI tools let one person do what required 5-10 in 2024
- YC funding solo founders more often (signal from W26 batch)
- "Cursor for PMs" demand is real — no product fills it
- Solo founder needs: measurement, strategy, customer dev, pricing — not just code

### Context engineering is the 2026 discipline
- If 2024 was RAG, 2025 was agentic, 2026 is context engines
- Building the minimal but complete information layer agents need to function
- The founders who understand scope of information = building the moats

### The death toll
- 14,000+ AI startups launched in 2024. 40% dead within 24 months ($2-50M raised included)
- Jasper AI acquired for parts. Copy.ai merged. Character.AI acquired for talent.
- Average AI subscription lifespan before churn: 4-6 months
- Only ~130 of thousands of claimed "AI agent" vendors are genuinely agentic
- "2025 was the year every vendor slapped 'agentic' on their homepage. 2026 is the year customers expect receipts."

### Funding reality
- AI = 41% of $128B VC on Carta — record high
- AI funding surged 85% in 2025 to $211B globally
- BUT: enterprise IT budgets growing at only 2% annually
- K-shaped market: hot thesis → oversubscribed, everything else → scraps
- Seed investors now expect $300-500K ARR before writing a check
- Top AI startups achieving $1M revenue per employee (Sequoia's threshold for market pull)
- 42% valuation premium for AI seed-stage vs non-AI

### Saturated categories (avoid)
- Coding assistants / copilots (Claude Code 75% small startup, Copilot 56% enterprise — game over)
- Consumer chatbots (diminishing returns from scaling)
- Content generation (commodity, race to zero — Jasper/Copy.ai are the case studies)
- Horizontal AI agents (half the entire market)
- Marketing automation (table stakes, no differentiation left)
- Generic "chat with your X" patterns

### Emerging categories (opportunities)
- Vertical AI agents (industry-specific: legal, healthcare, finance)
- AI evaluation/measurement infrastructure (enterprises can't measure ROI)
- Founder decision tools (YC 2026: "what to build, not how")
- Boring industry AI (YC: metal mills, government, field service)
- AI agencies with software margins (deliver finished work, not tools)
- Physical AI / vocational coaching (multimodal + wearables)
- Financial agent swarms (new quant strategies)
- Agent governance / "Know Your Agent" identity infrastructure
- Post-training tooling / LLM fine-tuning infrastructure
- Spatial/3D models as AI primitives

### What's being disrupted NOW
- Product analytics (Amplitude going agent-native)
- CI/CD monitoring (Faros, LinearB adding intelligence layer)
- Customer research tools (Looppanel, BuildBetter — but none code-integrated)
- Engineering metrics (DORA metrics being automated)
- Product management tools (Linear, Notion — but none measure value)

## Dev tools landscape (March 2026)

- Claude Code dominates small startups (75%). Copilot dominates enterprise (56%, procurement inertia).
- Market segmented: editor assistants (commoditized) vs. repository-level agents (differentiated)
- Editor assistant market is over as a startup opportunity — GitHub vs JetBrains vs Amazon, backed by infra companies
- Open space: repository-level orchestration, agent governance, CI/CD integration layer
- Claude Code's rise signals CLI-first, terminal-native tools aren't niche — they're where serious devs are

## Pricing in 2026
- SaaS fatigue is real — founders resist new subscriptions
- Usage-based pricing gaining (pay for what you use)
- Outcome-based winning (Intercom: $0.99/resolved ticket)
- Free tier expected — the question is what's behind the paywall
- Claude Code plugins are currently free — marketplace monetization coming
- $19-49/mo is the solo founder sweet spot (Linear $8, Notion $10, Vercel $20)

## New funding models

- Station F launched F/ai (backed by Sequoia, Mistral, OpenAI) — AI-specific accelerator
- Techstars matched YC: $220K including uncapped MFN SAFE

- Smart founders stack: cash accelerator + non-dilutive compute program

## Strategic implications

### For positioning
- Don't compete with code generation tools — compete with product thinking gaps
- "Measurement" is the wedge, but "product intelligence" is the category
- Distribution through Claude Code marketplace is the cheapest channel available right now
- Solo founders are the beachhead user — they have all the problems and no team to delegate to
- Frame as delivering work, not providing tools (Sequoia frame)
- Show production receipts, not demos (anti-agent-washing)

### For timing
- MCP adoption window: 6-12 months before consolidation
- Claude Code plugin marketplace: early enough to establish, growing fast enough to matter
- Verification-native tools: no established player yet
- Risk: Anthropic builds this themselves (high risk, 12-18 month window)
- Rescue engineering demand spike: vibe-coded products failing in production creates demand for quality tools

### For competitive response
- Default: "keep building." Override only with strong evidence.
- Someone launches similar: check if they have distribution. If no, irrelevant. If yes, differentiate or accelerate.
- Big tech enters space: focus on wedge use case they'll never prioritize. Solo founders are too small for Salesforce.

### For language
- Lead with outcomes and evidence, not capabilities and AI
- Name the specific workflow replaced, not the technology used
- "Receipts" = production evidence. Claims without receipts are agent washing.
- The swap test still works: if your copy works with a competitor's name, rewrite
