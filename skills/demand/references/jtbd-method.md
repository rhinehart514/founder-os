## Job Statement Format (Ulwick)

Verb + object. Solution-agnostic. Stable over time.

> "Get a leaking pipe fixed quickly"

NOT: "Find a plumber on Angi" (solution-specific, unstable).

Three types per job:
- **Functional**: "Answer every inbound call during business hours"
- **Emotional**: "Feel confident I'm not losing money to missed leads"
- **Social**: "Look like a professional operation when customers Google me"

## Outcome Statements

The measurable version. Format:
```
Minimize the [time / likelihood / number] of [negative outcome] when [context]
```

Examples:
- "Minimize the likelihood of losing a lead because nobody answered the phone"
- "Minimize the time it takes to follow up after giving an estimate"
- "Minimize the number of customers who hire a competitor because they answered first"

Outcomes are stable, measurable, and product-agnostic. Features change; outcomes don't.

## Job Map (8 Universal Stages)

Every functional job passes through these stages. Unmet needs cluster at specific stages:

1. **Define** — what needs to get done, criteria for success
2. **Locate** — find inputs, information, resources needed
3. **Prepare** — set up the environment, organize inputs
4. **Confirm** — verify readiness before executing
5. **Execute** — perform the core task
6. **Monitor** — track whether it's working
7. **Modify** — adjust when things change
8. **Conclude** — finish, clean up, hand off

Map customer outcomes to stages. Stages with the most high-importance, low-satisfaction outcomes = where to build.

## Opportunity Scoring (ODI)

Ulwick's formula:
```
Opportunity Score = Importance + max(Importance - Satisfaction, 0)
```

Both inputs: percentage of respondents rating 4 or 5 on a 1-5 scale.

| Score | Meaning | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| 10+ | Underserved | Build here. Premium pricing. |
| 7-9 | Moderately underserved | Viable opportunity. |
| <7 | Table stakes or overserved | Don't differentiate here. |

Survey questions:
- Importance: "When [job step], how important is it that you can [outcome]?" (1-5)
- Satisfaction: "Using [current solution], how satisfied are you with [outcome]?" (1-5)

Requires 150-600 respondents for statistical validity.

## Four Forces (Moesta)

Every switching decision has four forces:

```
PUSH (away from current)         PULL (toward new)
· Current pain / struggling      · Imagined better outcome
· Trigger event                  · Demo / proof it works
· Cost pressure                  · Peer adoption

HABIT (keep current)             ANXIETY (avoid new)
· "This is how I work"           · Fear of data loss
· Sunk cost / contracts          · "Too busy to set up"
· Familiar workflow              · "What if it breaks?"
```

**Switch happens when: PUSH + PULL > HABIT + ANXIETY**

Product design:
- Amplify push (make them feel the pain they're ignoring)
- Increase pull (demo the outcome, not features)
- Reduce anxiety (done-for-you setup, free trial, no contract)
- Break habits (make new way invisible — zero behavior change required)

Timeline technique (Moesta): work backwards through 4 stages:
1. First Thought — when did you first realize something needed to change?
2. Passive Looking — what did you notice without actively searching?
3. Active Looking — when did you start comparing options?
4. Deciding Moment — what tipped the decision?

## Identifying Jobs

Three sources, ranked by reliability:

1. **Spending** — what are they already paying for? Money trails don't lie.
2. **Non-consumption** — what job goes undone? Biggest opportunities hide here.
3. **Switching moments** — when do they hire/fire tools? Maximum frustration = the job.

## Ranking

Two axes: **Dollar Impact** × **Underservedness**

- High-dollar + unserved = gold
- High-dollar + underserved = strong
- Low-dollar + well-served = trap

## Early-Stage Adaptation

Ulwick's ODI requires 150+ survey respondents. Pre-product adaptation:

- **Importance**: founder's assessment from 5+ qualitative interviews
- **Satisfaction**: assessment of how well current alternatives handle it
- **Flag explicitly**: "qualitative estimate, not survey data"

This gives directional signal, not statistical validity. Good enough for product design decisions; not good enough for investor decks.
