# Customer Language Library

Real words solo founders and indie hackers use. Sourced from Indie Hackers, Hacker News,
DEV Community, and founder blogs. Use these exact phrases in copy — they're how the customer
talks, not how marketers talk.

## Pain Language (what they say when describing their problems)

### "I can't tell if my product is good"
- "I spent three weeks on a feature and genuinely couldn't tell if it was brilliant or stupid."
- "Is it me? Is it the product? Or is it just not meant to work?"
- "It's really hard to know with 100% conviction whether what we're doing is useful."
- "no one told me I was focused on the wrong thing"

### "I'm building in circles"
- "spinning on ideas and not shipping anything"
- "I've been working on an MVP for months now and keep changing platforms"
- "got lost in working on rabbit hole features"
- "jumping from tool to tool"
- "thrashing between too many directions"
- "my backlogs are full, sort of sorted by some perceived importance (totally adhoc)"
- "debating between features, user feedback, marketing, and random (but sometimes good) ideas"

### "I'm building the wrong thing"
- "I was building features nobody asked for instead of talking to the 12 people who were actually using the product."
- "Commit velocity isn't the same as user-facing progress."
- "So we keep building more features, hoping that this next one will be the thing that makes people care."
- "More code. Less feedback. No traction."
- "Building feels like progress. Selling feels like rejection. So we optimise our stack instead of having one actual conversation."
- "It's easier to configure CI/CD than it is to cold email 10 potential customers."

### "Nobody cares / the silence"
- "What almost broke me was the silence."
- "Nobody checks, nobody cares."
- "No signups. No traction. Maybe a few comments, mostly from your friends."
- "We ship to the sound of crickets because we conflated building with launching."
- "Marketing Feels Like Shouting Into the Void"

### "I'm alone with this"
- "not having anyone who understands and is invested as much as you are"
- "no co-founder to brainstorm with"
- "It's wild how a single negative comment can spiral into 'I should shut this down' when you're alone with it at 2am."
- "the loneliness was probably the hardest part, especially when friends don't get why"

### "Decision fatigue"
- "it's easy to get stuck in decision loops — should I add this feature or that one?"
- "Stuck planning / Overwhelmed by execution / Watching time slip"
- "Context Switching Will Destroy Your Brain"

## Desire Language (what they say when describing what they want)

- "We did weekly 30-min calls. Dead simple — what'd you ship, what's next, what's stuck." (accountability)
- "I'd rather have freedom than a fancy job title."
- "I'm not into 10x-ing and world domination; I'm into paying rent and enjoying my day."
- "Know your enough."
- "You don't need to grow fast. You just need to grow consistently."
- "Sticking with one project and improving it, even when growth is painfully slow, seems to produce more consistent results"

**What they actually want (synthesized):**
1. Someone who checks on them (accountability)
2. High-context feedback from someone who's invested (not generic advice)
3. Knowing if they're building the right thing (validation > velocity)
4. Freedom and sustainability, not unicorn ambitions

## Evaluation Language (how they know if something is working)

- "The gap is never technical — it is understanding why people leave in the first 2 minutes."
- "Real progress rarely feels dramatic in the moment."
- "None of it makes for a good tweet. But all of it matters."
- "The channel that yielded the most sales was one-on-one connections through Zoom... Actually 100% of those turned into a sale"
- "I launched and made six sales at 50 each. It's not much, but validates the need."

## Celebration Language (what they're proud of — use for social proof framing)

- "this small $150 means a lot to me than the fat paycheck I received at my day job"
- "I jumped like crazy in my bedroom"
- "I wasn't launching into the void anymore."
- "I continued to launch and continued to flop, but I never considered giving up."

**Milestones the community cares about:**
1. First dollar (proof of existence, not profit)
2. First paying customer (someone chose this)
3. First $1K MRR
4. $10K MRR
5. Product Hunt #1 Product of the Day
6. HN front page

## AI Fatigue Language (how they talk about AI tool burnout — use for differentiation)

### The 70% problem (named concept in developer culture)
- "it can get to 70% of the task, but you don't have the knowledge required to complete it, that's pretty much the same as 0%"
- "every time you start a new conversation with him, it's like his first day on the job"
- "supremely confident, always says yes" but "makes very critical mistakes in the difficult stuff"

### AI brain rot / loss of ownership
- "I literally felt myself getting AI brain rot... felt like I started losing brain cells"
- "It robs me of the feeling of achievement... It's not me who figured it out so it's not my achievement"
- "coding feels less fun... I'm not doing so much of the thinking for myself"
- "I stopped caring. Code reviews became rubber stamps."

### AI fatigue (cognitive overload, not productivity loss)
- "I shipped more code last quarter than any quarter in my career. I also felt more drained than any quarter in my career."
- "AI reduces the cost of production but increases the cost of coordination, review, and decision-making."
- "I became a reviewer. A judge. A quality inspector on an assembly line that never stops."
- "you are collaborating with a probabilistic system, and your brain is wired for deterministic ones."

### Vibe coding backlash
- "things start off fast, but once you need stateful logic, secure auth, or just clean handoff between components... things collapse"
- "people come to us with half-working codebases duct-taped together by AI, and they can't even onboard their first user"
- "It made it cheaper to create software. It did not make it cheaper to own software."
- "almost-right code" — top frustration (66% of developers in vibe coding survey)
- METR study (July 2025): experienced developers using AI took 19% longer, despite believing they were 20% faster

### Tool abandonment
- "just another month of 'trying things' and hoping something sticks"
- "No real growth. No measurable ROI. No clarity."
- "If your systems are broken, AI will break them faster."
- "by the time I finish crafting the prompt and execute, it takes me about 8 minutes... if I have to re-prompt, 10-12 minutes"
- "I don't trust it to write anything big, and don't need it to write anything small"

## How to Use This in Copy

### The core insight
The dominant anxiety is NOT "is this technically correct" — it's "does this matter to anyone."
Pain clusters around silence, invisibility, and building without feedback. Not technical failure.

### Words that resonate (use these)
- accountability, high-context feedback, knowing what matters
- "building the right thing" (not "building things right")
- silence, crickets, void (for problem framing)
- first dollar, first customer, first sign someone cares (for aspiration)
- freedom, sustainability, enough (for values alignment)

### Words that repel (avoid these)
- 10x, productivity, efficiency, scale (AI fatigue triggers)
- AI-powered, intelligent, smart (burned-out language)
- ship faster, move faster, velocity (speed is not the problem)
- disrupt, revolutionize, transform (hollow)

### The positioning gap
Founders say: "I can't tell if my product is good. Nobody tells me I'm focused on the wrong thing."
No tool answers this. Analytics tools require users (which they don't have yet). AI coding tools
produce more code (which isn't the problem). The gap is judgment, not output.
