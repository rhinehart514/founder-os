# Positioning Frameworks

## Category Creation

When no existing category fits your product, create one. The goal is to make the listener understand what you do by defining a new space.

**Formula:** "[Familiar thing] for [specific audience]" or "[Familiar thing] that [key difference]"

**Examples:**
- "Cursor for Product Managers" (YC's framing)
- "A cofounder that measures" (founder-os positioning)

**When to use:** When existing categories are saturated or misleading. When comparison to existing tools undervalues your product.

**Risk:** Nobody searches for a category that doesn't exist yet. Need content to define the space.

## Competitor Wedge

Position against a specific, well-known competitor. Define yourself by what they get wrong.

**Formula:** "[Competitor] but [key difference]" or "Unlike [competitor], we [difference]"

**Rules:**
- Only wedge against competitors your user already knows
- The difference must be observable in 30 seconds
- Never attack — contrast

**When to use:** When a dominant player exists and your user is frustrated with them.

## "Only For..." Framing

Narrow your audience explicitly. Counter-intuitive but effective: excluding people makes the included feel chosen.

**Formula:** "Built for [specific person]. Not for [everyone else]."

**Examples:**
- "For solo founders who ship. Not for teams with sprint planning."
- "For developers who want to know if their product got better. Not for developers who want clean code."

**When to use:** When your product genuinely serves a niche better than horizontal tools.

**Risk:** Limits TAM. That's the point — at stage one, TAM doesn't matter.

## Problem-Centric

Lead with the pain, not the solution. The reader recognizes the problem and reads on.

**Formula:** "[Problem statement]. [Product] does [solution]."

**Rules:**
- The problem must be something the reader says out loud (not something you tell them they have)
- Use their words, not yours (from customer-intel)

## Outcome-Centric

Lead with what's different after using the product. Skip the problem — go straight to the result.

**Formula:** "[Measurable outcome]. [How it works — one sentence]."

**Rules:**
- The outcome must be specific ("save time" = garbage, "know if your product got better in one command" = specific)
- Must be verifiable (the user can confirm it happened)

## Sell the Work, Not the Tool (2026)

The defining positioning frame of 2026. From Sequoia: "If you sell the tool, you're in a race
against the model. If you sell the work, every improvement in the model makes your service faster,
cheaper, and harder to compete with."

**Formula:** "We [do the finished work]. You [review/approve/use the result]."

**Examples:**
- "We evaluate your product. You get the score." (not "a tool to evaluate your product")
- "We research your market. You get the brief." (not "a research assistant")
- Corvera (YC W26): "AI agent workforce for CPG back-office" — not "AI tools for CPG"

**When to use:** Always consider this frame first in 2026. The copilot/assistant frame is 2023.
If the product can be described as delivering finished work rather than assisting with work, use this.

**The test:** Does your copy position the product as a tool the user operates, or as a colleague
that delivers results? "Tool you operate" = commodity race. "Colleague that delivers" = service moat.

## Bounded Autonomy (Enterprise Trust)

For enterprise or cautious buyers. Signal that the AI acts independently BUT within defined limits.

**Formula:** "[Agent does X] within [governance boundary]. [Escalation path] when [uncertainty]."

**When to use:** When the buyer's objection is trust/control, not capability. Enterprise sales,
regulated industries, high-stakes workflows.

## Evidence-First (Anti-Agent-Washing)

2026 buyers have seen too many "agentic" claims with no production evidence. Lead with receipts.

**Formula:** "[Specific metric from production]. [What the product does]. [Social proof if available]."

**Examples:**
- "Corvera: $0 to $33K MRR in 4 weeks" (not "AI-powered back-office automation")
- "Cardinal: 40+ YC companies paying before Demo Day" (not "the future of [category]")

**When to use:** When competing against vaporware or when the category is saturated with claims.
The antidote to agent washing is evidence.
