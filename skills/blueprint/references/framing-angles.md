# Seven Framing Archetypes

Every product can be framed through multiple angles. Generate at least 3, test all against the peer test.

## 1. Cost Anchor
> "A $70K office manager for $199/mo"

Anchors to a cost they already understand. Works when displacement math is dramatic.
**Strength**: instant ROI clarity. **Risk**: commoditizes you to a cost comparison.

## 2. Outcome Anchor
> "Never miss another call"

Anchors to a result they want. Works when the outcome is felt daily.
**Strength**: emotional, memorable. **Risk**: sounds like every ad ever if not specific enough.

## 3. Role Anchor
> "Your $199 office manager"

Anchors to a human role they'd hire. Works when the product replaces a person's job.
**Strength**: tangible, relatable. **Risk**: sets expectations for human-level flexibility.

## 4. Category Anchor
> "AI front office for trades"

Creates or claims a category. Works when you can afford to educate the market.
**Strength**: differentiating, ownable. **Risk**: nobody searches for a category that doesn't exist yet.

## 5. Displacement Anchor
> "Cancel Angi. Cancel your answering service. Cancel your SEO agency."

Anchors to tools they're already paying for. Works when the product replaces multiple vendors.
**Strength**: concrete savings, actionable. **Risk**: defines you by what you replace, not what you are.

## 6. Aspiration Anchor
> "Your business runs without you"

Anchors to who they want to become. Works as a retention/loyalty frame (month 6+).
**Strength**: powerful vision, shareable. **Risk**: too good to be true for acquisition — needs proof first.

## 7. Fear Anchor
> "Every missed call is $285 gone"

Anchors to loss aversion. Works when the pain is quantifiable and recurring.
**Strength**: urgency, hard to ignore. **Risk**: negative framing can feel aggressive.

## The Peer Test

For each frame, write what the customer would say to a peer:
- At a networking meeting (30 seconds)
- To a colleague in the same industry
- When asked "what do you use for [category]?"

If the sentence doesn't flow naturally in any of these, the framing is wrong.

## Context Matching

Different frames for different moments:
- **Acquisition** (ad, landing page, cold pitch): Cost, Outcome, or Fear
- **Consideration** (demo, trial): Role or Displacement
- **Retention** (month 3+): Aspiration
- **Referral** (word of mouth): whatever they naturally say — often Outcome or Aspiration
