# Switch Interview Guide

Based on Bob Moesta's demand-side sales method. Generates customer interview scripts for discovering jobs, forces, and switching triggers.

## The Timeline Technique

Work BACKWARDS through the customer's decision journey. Don't ask what they think — ask what they DID.

### Phase 1: The Deciding Moment
- "Walk me through the day you decided to [sign up / switch / buy]. What happened?"
- "What were you doing right before that?"
- "Was there a specific moment or event that made you say 'I need to do something about this'?"

### Phase 2: Active Looking
- "Before you decided, what did you look at? Who did you talk to?"
- "What were you comparing? What were the alternatives?"
- "What almost stopped you from switching?"

### Phase 3: Passive Looking
- "Before you started actively looking, was there a period where you noticed things but didn't act?"
- "What did you notice? Ads, conversations, competitors?"
- "Why didn't you act sooner?"

### Phase 4: First Thought
- "When was the very first time you thought 'this isn't working'?"
- "What was happening at that moment?"
- "How long ago was that?"

## Four Forces Questions

### Push (away from current)
- "What was the most frustrating thing about [current solution]?"
- "Tell me about the last time [current solution] really let you down."
- "How much were you spending on [current solution]? Did it feel worth it?"

### Pull (toward new)
- "What did you imagine would be different with [new solution]?"
- "Was there a specific feature or promise that got your attention?"
- "Did you see someone else using something that made you think 'I want that'?"

### Anxiety (blocking the switch)
- "What almost stopped you from switching?"
- "What were you worried would go wrong?"
- "Was there anything you were afraid of losing?"

### Habit (keeping the status quo)
- "What were you doing before that mostly worked?"
- "Was there anything about the old way that was actually good?"
- "How long had you been doing it the old way?"

## Moesta's Key Technique

**Ask "what is it NOT?"** — people eliminate options more easily than they describe what they want.

- "Of the things you looked at, which did you reject first? Why?"
- "What would have been a dealbreaker?"
- "What did [competitor] get wrong?"

## Generating the Interview Script

When generating a script for a specific customer:

1. Adapt the timeline phases to their context (what are they switching FROM?)
2. Name specific competing solutions in the questions
3. Include industry-specific language they'd use
4. Keep it to 15-20 questions max (30 min interview)
5. Group by timeline phase, not by force (feels more natural)
6. End with: "Is there anything I should have asked but didn't?"

## After the Interview

Map findings to:
- Job statements (what progress were they trying to make?)
- Four forces (what pushed/pulled/blocked/held them?)
- Outcome statements (what would "done well" look like?)
- Competing alternatives (what were they actually choosing between?)
