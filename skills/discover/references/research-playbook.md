# Research Playbook

Specific search patterns that reliably surface non-obvious signal.
Run these before generating ideas — not after.

**founder-os integration:** These searches use WebSearch. Run all 4 before spawning
explorer/market-analyst/customer agents. Feed the synthesis to agents as context.

---

## Minimum search set (run all 4 before generating)

| Goal | Search pattern |
|---|---|
| What's table stakes | `"[product type]" features site:producthunt.com` |
| What users actually want | `"[product/category]" reddit frustrations OR "wish it had" OR "missing feature"` |
| What best-in-class looks like | `[competitor] changelog` or `[competitor] what's new` |
| What's emerging | `[product area] new startup` or `[job-to-be-done] new tool` |

---

## Deeper cuts (use when session warrants it)

**Competitive intelligence**
- `[competitor] pricing page` — reveals what they think users pay for
- `[competitor] G2 reviews` or `[competitor] app store reviews` — unfiltered user voice
- `[competitor] YC OR "product hunt" OR "launched"` — recent entrants to the space

**Failure research**
- `"[feature idea]" failed OR discontinued OR sunset`
- `why [product type] users churn`
- `[competitor] negative reviews`

**Analogous spaces**
The most valuable research is often outside the direct category. Find the product that
does the underlying job best — regardless of industry — and look at how they handle it.
- `best [underlying job] product design`
- `[analogous industry] UX patterns`

**AI displacement check**
For any information-processing feature, search what's already being handled by AI
in adjacent products. The question isn't whether to add AI — it's whether the feature
still needs to be a feature at all.
- `[feature area] AI` or `[product type] AI native`

---

## What to do with research findings

Don't dump raw results. Synthesize into 3-5 sentences:
- What's already standard in the market
- What the most interesting recent entrant is doing differently
- What users say they wish existed
- What AI is changing about the space

Then generate ideas from *that* base, not from memory.
